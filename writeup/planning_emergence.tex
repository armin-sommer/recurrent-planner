\begin{abstract}
\noindent Model-free RL on a state-indexed relational recurrent core induces \emph{planning}, and we
identify the mechanism precisely: the recurrent ``thinking'' loop performs \textbf{multi-step value
propagation} --- \textbf{amortized policy evaluation} of a goal-directed value field along the
environment's transition graph $\NN$ --- and the policy is improved by a single one-step lookahead at
the actor head. It is \emph{not} value iteration (the per-tick aggregation is a convex average, with no
$\max$ over actions), \emph{not} a successor representation (the goal enters the iterate), and \emph{not}
trajectory search (no multi-step plan is stored). On a 300M-step Sokoban model we show, causally and on
held-out levels, that (i)~value propagation is multi-step and \emph{deepens with thinking}; (ii)~the
per-tick operator is a learned, graph-respecting, goal-anchored kernel that \emph{generalizes} (not
per-level memorization); and (iii)~information routes \emph{along} $\NN$, around walls. The relational
structure is emergent --- no locality mask is imposed.
\end{abstract}

\section{The model, and a mathematical model of one tick}

The core is a state-indexed attention-LSTM: each latent cell is the $C{=}32$-channel vector at a board
square $s$ over $S{=}H{\times}W$ tokens. Per thinking tick every cell attends over the whole board (dense,
4 heads, \emph{sparse} 1.5-entmax normalizer), aggregates neighbour values, and applies an LSTM gate;
depth $D{=}3$, up to $K{=}6$ ticks, trained 300M IMPALA steps with \emph{no} locality mask. Latents bind
to states (tile type is linearly decodable from $h(s)$ at $0.70$--$0.77$, chance $0.5$, flat over ticks).

Linearizing the value channel of one weight-tied tick gives the mechanism in closed form:
\begin{equation}
c \;\leftarrow\; \gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}\,A\,c + r_{\mathrm{eff}}
\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad
c^\star=(I-\gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}A)^{-1}r_{\mathrm{eff}}=\sum_{i\ge0}(\gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}A)^{i}\,r_{\mathrm{eff}},
\label{eq:resolvent}
\end{equation}
the discounted \textbf{policy-evaluation resolvent}. The entmax attention $A$ (row-stochastic, supported
on $\NN$) plays $\gamma P^{\pi}$; the per-channel forget gate $\sigma(g_f)$ plays the discount; the
input injection $[x;\text{prev}]W_{\mathrm{in}}$ plays the reward source $r$. Crucially the aggregation
$z=(A\,v)\,W_{\mathrm{out}}=\mathbb{E}_{s'\sim A}[\cdot]$ is a \emph{convex average} --- there is
\textbf{no $\max_a$} --- so the loop \emph{evaluates} a (fixed, near-greedy) policy. The $\max$
(improvement) is a single $\arg\max_a[r_a+\gamma V(s'_a)]$ applied at the actor head. The loop thus
realizes \emph{one step of generalized policy iteration}: evaluate in the loop, improve at the head.

\section{Evidence}

\subsection{Value propagation is multi-step and deepens with thinking}

Perturbing a cell $d$ hops from the agent, its causal effect on the agent's latent \emph{arrives later
for larger $d$} and builds up over ticks (Fig.~\ref{fig:reach}). A single tick reaches only $\sim$3 hops,
yet cells $7$--$9$ hops away still influence the agent --- starting near zero and growing over $\sim$4
ticks. Since one tick cannot span $8$ hops, the intermediate cells must relay the signal: the cells a
latent pulls from are \emph{themselves} updated each tick, so reach \emph{compounds} (genuine multi-step
iteration), filling the board within $\sim$3--4 ticks. Consistently, the converged value is a recursive
Bellman fixed point (optimality residual$/\mathrm{std}(V)$ flat at $[0.19,0.25,0.25,0.22]$ over greedy
successors $0$--$3$), and the policy's agreement with one-step value-lookahead rises with thinking
($0.34\!\to\!0.41$; chance $0.25$).

\subsection{A learned, generalizable propagation algorithm}

Where does a cell pull value \emph{from}? Not merely its neighbours: attention mass decays geometrically
with \emph{graph} distance ($\rho\approx0.66$, effective reach $\sim$3 hops), with $34\%$ of mass beyond
one hop, essentially \emph{zero} mass through walls, and a direct $\sim$$0.07$ link to the goal cell
(Fig.~\ref{fig:kernel}). This single kernel is applied across unseen levels and adapts \emph{causally} to
novel wall and goal edits rather than retrieving a per-level answer --- a generalizable value-propagation
algorithm, not memorization. Relocating the goal reshapes the propagated latent more than a matched
transition change ($1.55\times$), confirming the goal enters the iterate (policy evaluation), not a
reward-agnostic successor representation.

\subsection{It respects the transition graph}

The propagation is causally tied to $\NN$. A perturbation blocked by a wall moves the agent latent only
$0.38\times$ as much as an open one at matched pixel distance (Fig.~\ref{fig:walls}; partial correlation
of influence with graph-distance, controlling Euclidean, $=-0.32$). Information routes \emph{around}
walls, along the transition graph --- and this emerges with no locality mask imposed.

\section{What it is, and what it is not}

The thinking loop is \textbf{amortized, graph-respecting policy evaluation}: it propagates a goal-directed
value field along $\NN$ via a fixed, wide, weight-tied operator (the attention is stationary and does not
sharpen; effective horizon $\sim$9 ticks, so the long range is compiled into the weights and the loop
runs a few refinement sweeps), and the policy is improved by a single lookahead at the head. It is
therefore planning by \textbf{value propagation} --- definitively \emph{not} value iteration (no
$\max_a$ in the loop), \emph{not} successor-representation construction (the goal is in the iterate), and
\emph{not} trajectory or tree search (only the immediate action $a_0$ is decodable; $a_1{\dots}a_5$ sit
at chance). Planning emerges from RL, over latents that bind to states and route along the environment's
transition graph.
