\title{\vspace{-1.2em}\bf Planning by Value Propagation\\[2pt]
\large Binding, a relational world model, and amortized policy evaluation on Sokoban}
\author{Recurrent-Planner project}
\date{\vspace{-0.6em}\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\vspace{-2.2em}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent Model-free RL on a state-indexed relational recurrent core induces \emph{planning}, via a
three-step chain that emerges with \emph{no} locality mask: (1)~latent cells \textbf{bind} to environment
states; (2)~their learned routing passes information along the one-step transition graph $\NN$ --- a
\textbf{relational world model}; and (3)~the recurrent ``thinking'' loop \textbf{plans} by multi-step
value propagation, i.e.\ \textbf{amortized policy evaluation} (not value iteration --- the aggregation is
a convex average, with no $\max$ over actions; the improvement is a one-step lookahead at the actor head).
All three are shown causally, on held-out levels, in a 300M-step Sokoban model.
\end{abstract}

\vspace{-0.8em}
\section*{Setup}
\vspace{-2pt}
The core is a state-indexed attention-LSTM: each latent is the $C{=}32$ vector at a board square $s$ over
$S{=}H{\times}W$ tokens. Per thinking tick every cell attends over the whole board (\emph{dense}, no mask;
4 heads; sparse 1.5-entmax), aggregates neighbour values, and applies an LSTM gate. Depth $D{=}3$, up to
$K{=}6$ ticks, trained 300M IMPALA steps.

\section{Binding: the latents are the states}
\vspace{-2pt}
A linear probe recovers the tile type at square $s$ from its latent $h(s)$ well above chance for every
object class, including the rare ones (box/target/agent are $1$--$4\%$ of squares). Binding is flat across
ticks --- read from the input embedding, not built by the loop.

\section{A relational world model: routing along $\NN$}
\vspace{-2pt}
With no locality mask, the learned attention routes information along the one-step transition graph
$\NN$: its mass concentrates on the (wall-masked) move-reachable neighbours at $7$--$13\times$ chance.
The relation is \emph{causal} and respects walls --- a perturbation \emph{blocked} by a wall shifts the
agent's latent only $0.38\times$ as much as an \emph{open} one at matched pixel distance
(Fig.~\ref{fig:walls}; partial correlation of influence with graph-distance, controlling Euclidean,
$=-0.32$). Information flows \emph{around} walls, so the latent relations implement the environment's
transition model.

\section{Planning: amortized policy evaluation (value propagation)}
\vspace{-2pt}
The loop plans over this world model by \emph{propagating value}. Linearizing the value channel of one
weight-tied tick gives the mechanism in closed form:
\vspace{-2pt}
\[
c \;\leftarrow\; \gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}\,A\,c + r_{\mathrm{eff}}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
c^\star=(I-\gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}A)^{-1}r_{\mathrm{eff}}=\sum_{i\ge0}(\gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}A)^{i}\,r_{\mathrm{eff}},
\]
the discounted \textbf{policy-evaluation resolvent}: the entmax attention $A$ (row-stochastic, on $\NN$)
plays $\gamma P^{\pi}$, the forget gate $\sigma(g_f)$ the discount, the input injection the reward source
$r$. The aggregation $z=(A v)W_{\mathrm{out}}=\mathbb{E}_{s'\sim A}[\cdot]$ is a \emph{convex average} ---
no $\max_a$ --- so the loop \emph{evaluates}; the $\max$ (improvement) is a single
$\arg\max_a[r_a+\gamma V(s'_a)]$ at the head.

This propagation is \emph{multi-step and deepens with thinking}: a perturbation $d$ hops from the agent
reaches it only after several ticks, building from $\approx0$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:reach}). One tick spans
$\sim$3 hops, yet cells $7$--$9$ away still arrive --- so intermediate cells relay the signal: the cells a
latent pulls from are \emph{themselves} updated, and reach \emph{compounds}. Where it pulls value from is
a learned, graph-respecting, goal-anchored kernel that \emph{generalizes} (Fig.~\ref{fig:kernel}:
geometric decay $\rho{\approx}0.66$, a third of the mass beyond one hop, $0$ through walls, $+0.07$ direct
to the goal; it adapts causally to novel goal/wall edits --- not per-level memorization). The converged
value is a recursive Bellman fixed point (optimality residual$/\mathrm{std}(V)$ flat at
$[0.19,0.25,0.25,0.22]$ over greedy successors $0$--$3$); the policy increasingly matches one-step
value-lookahead as it thinks ($0.34{\to}0.41$, chance $0.25$).

\paragraph{The value field tracks the task and the physics, and re-plans.} Causal interventions confirm a
genuine policy-evaluation field (Fig.~\ref{fig:abl}). Relocating the goal or a box to the far side shifts
the value in the resolvent-predicted direction --- higher as the goal nears the agent or a box nears a
target ($\mathrm{corr}=+0.18/+0.16$, $n{=}256$) --- and a box move moves it \emph{more} than a goal move
($1.5\sigma$ vs $1.0\sigma$), as the box is the task-critical object. The field also adopts new
\emph{physics}: a wall that lengthens the agent's route lowers the value ($94\%$ of cases, $-1.3\sigma$),
far more than a matched \emph{off-path} wall ($-0.35\sigma$), and this consequence \emph{builds over
thinking ticks}. Crucially it reaches the agent's \emph{own} square ($2.2\times$ an off-path wall, again
building over ticks) and re-plans its move ($3\times$ the action-change rate of a cosmetic wall). The model
does this by propagating the consequence as value \emph{content} through its fixed, graph-respecting kernel
--- not by re-routing attention.

\vspace{-4pt}
\section*{Conclusion}
\vspace{-2pt}
Planning emerges from RL as \emph{one step of generalized policy iteration split across the architecture}:
amortized, graph-respecting \textbf{policy evaluation} in the recurrent loop (the long horizon compiled
into the weights; a few refinement sweeps at inference) plus a one-step \textbf{lookahead improvement} at
the head --- over latents that bind to states (\S1) and route along the transition graph $\NN$ (\S2). It
is planning by value propagation: \emph{not} value iteration, \emph{not} successor-representation
construction, and \emph{not} trajectory or tree search (only the immediate action is decodable).

\section{Architecture and training}
\vspace{-2pt}
\textbf{Core.} A state-indexed attention-LSTM. The recurrent carry is, per stacked cell $d\in\{0,1,2\}$
($D{=}3$), a cell memory $c_d$ and hidden $h_d$, each in $\mathbb{R}^{S\times C}$ over $S{=}H{\times}W{=}100$
board squares with $C{=}32$ channels; $x\in\mathbb{R}^{S\times C}$ is a convolutional board embedding, fixed
across ticks. Each thinking tick applies, for $d=0,1,2$ bottom-to-top (with $\mathrm{prev}$ the hidden of
the cell below, or the top hidden from the previous tick for $d{=}0$), the weight-tied update
\vspace{-3pt}
\begin{align*}
u &= \mathrm{RMSNorm}\!\big(h_d + [x;\mathrm{prev}]W_{\mathrm{in}}^d + b^d\big)\odot s^d,\\
q,k,v &= u W_q^d,\; u W_k^d,\; u W_v^d \quad(\text{4 heads},\ d_h{=}8),\\
A &= \mathrm{entmax}_{1.5}\!\big(qk^{\top}/\sqrt{d_h} + \mathrm{RelBias}^d\big),\qquad z=(A\,v)\,W_{\mathrm{out}}^d,\\
[g_i,g_j,g_f,g_o] &= [x;\mathrm{prev};z]\,W_g^d + b_g^d,\\
c_d &\leftarrow c_d\,\sigma(g_f) + \tanh(g_i)\,\sigma(g_j),\qquad h_d \leftarrow \tanh(c_d)\odot\tanh(g_o).
\end{align*}
\vspace{-6pt}

Attention is \emph{dense} (no locality mask) with a sparse $1.5$-entmax normalizer; the aggregation
$z=(A v)W_{\mathrm{out}}$ is a convex average (no $\max$). Up to $K{=}6$ ticks. The per-board readout pools
$m=\mathrm{ReLU}\big((h_2+x)W_{\mathrm{dense}}+b\big)$ over the grid into actor logits $mW_a+b_a$ and a
critic value $V=(mW_c+b_c)\,s_{\mathrm{head}}$.

\textbf{Training.} IMPALA on Boxoban unfiltered-train, $300$M steps, $\gamma{=}0.97$, variable thinking
depth $d\sim\mathcal{U}\{1..6\}$ per rollout (the learner replays the same depth). Online train solve
$\approx83\%$, \texttt{valid\_medium} $\approx31\%$. All probes use the held-out \texttt{valid\_medium}
split at the $300$M checkpoint.

\textbf{Faithful recompute.} The model's own forward hits a tracer error on the offset-tied
\texttt{rel\_bias} gather under \texttt{nn.scan}; every probe instead recomputes the $D{=}3$ entmax stack in
plain JAX from the loaded parameters (\texttt{recompute\_d3}), validated to reproduce the model's final
hidden state to $1.8\times10^{-7}$.

\section{Experiments}
\vspace{-2pt}
Scripts are under \texttt{results/}; all on held-out \texttt{valid\_medium} at the $300$M checkpoint.

\textbf{Step 1 --- binding.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt,topsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{Binding probe} (\texttt{interp\_planning\_d3.py}): linear probe $h(s)\!\to\!$ tile type,
per-object balanced accuracy (chance $0.5$) --- wall/box/target/agent $=0.77/0.76/0.70/0.75$, flat over ticks.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Step 2 --- relational world model (routing along $\NN$).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt,topsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{Recovery of $\NN$} (\texttt{interp\_planning\_d3.py}): attention mass on the wall-masked
one-step move-neighbours vs uniform chance --- $7$--$13\times$; entmax support broad ($\sim$44--88 keys).
\item \textbf{Through-walls} (\texttt{interp\_wall\_d3.py}): flip a floor cell to a wall at matched pixel
distance; agent-latent influence for graph-far (blocked) vs graph-near (open) cells --- blocked
$=0.38\times$ open, partial corr$(\text{infl},\text{graph}\!\mid\!\text{Euclid})=-0.32$.
\item \textbf{Learned kernel} (\texttt{interp\_e4\_d3.py}): attention mass vs graph distance --- geometric
decay $\rho{\approx}0.66$ (reach $\sim$3 hops), $34\%$ beyond one hop, $0.00$ to graph-unreachable cells,
$+0.07$ directly on the goal cell.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Step 3 --- planning by value propagation.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt,topsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{No-max / stationary operator} (config + \texttt{interp\_e1\_d3.py}): trained config is
\texttt{readout="softmax"} (convex average), \texttt{maxplus} off; $A$ is stationary and does not sharpen
(top-1 mass ${\approx}0.16$, ${\sim}85$ keys, $\cos(A_t,A_{t-1})\!\to\!0.995$) $\Rightarrow$ evaluation, not iteration.
\item \textbf{Reach compounds} (\texttt{interp\_e5\_d3.py}): perturb a cell $d$ hops away; agent-influence
onset per tick --- far cells ($d{=}7$--$9$) arrive only after ${\sim}3$--$4$ ticks (from ${\approx}0$); horizon grows.
\item \textbf{Bellman self-consistency} (\texttt{interp\_bellman\_d3.py}): optimality residual
$|V-\max_a(r{+}\gamma V(s'))|/\mathrm{std}(V)$ at agent and greedy successors $0$--$3$ $=[0.19,0.25,0.25,0.22]$
(low, flat) $\Rightarrow$ recursive fixed point.
\item \textbf{Lookahead-consistency} (\texttt{interp\_lookahead\_d3.py}): policy agreement with
$\arg\max_a[r_a{+}\gamma V(s'_a)]$ over its own value $=0.41$ (chance $0.25$), rising $0.34\!\to\!0.41$ with depth.
\item \textbf{Reward-relabel} (\texttt{interp\_e2\_d3.py}): move the goal (keep $P$) vs a matched wall-flip
(change $P$); far-field latent shift $0.35$ vs $0.22$ (ratio $1.55$) $\Rightarrow$ goal in the iterate
(policy evaluation, not a successor representation).
\item \textbf{Far-side interventions} (\texttt{interp\_e6b\_d3.py}): relocate goal/box far ---
$\mathrm{corr}(\Delta V,-\Delta\text{dist})=+0.18/{+}0.16$; box impact $1.5\sigma>$ goal $1.0\sigma$.
\item \textbf{Operator adaptation} (\texttt{interp\_e7\_d3.py}, \texttt{interp\_e7b\_d3.py}): $A$ is
near-invariant under edits (row-change near/far ratio $1.1\times$; neighbours of a new wall keep attending to
it) $\Rightarrow$ no routing re-wire.
\item \textbf{Value content} (\texttt{interp\_e8\_d3.py}): value-projection norm $\|v\|$, wall/floor $=0.94$,
floor$\to$wall flip ratio $1.06$ $\Rightarrow$ walls are not ``dead''.
\item \textbf{New physics} (\texttt{interp\_e9\_d3.py}): a path-blocking wall lowers the value ($94\%$,
$-1.3\sigma$) vs an off-path wall ($-0.35\sigma$); the effect builds over ticks $\Rightarrow$ the value
adopts the new transition structure.
\item \textbf{Reaches the agent + re-plans} (\texttt{interp\_e10\_d3.py}): a path-blocking wall outside the
agent's view shifts the agent's \emph{own} latent $2.2\times$ an off-path wall (building over ticks) and
changes its greedy action $3\times$ more often.
\end{itemize}

\noindent Together: the loop propagates a goal-directed value field through a fixed, graph-respecting kernel
(policy evaluation); interventions show the value field tracks task (goal/box) and physics (wall) changes,
reaches the agent, and re-plans --- by propagating value \emph{content}, not by re-routing.
